Support the work

Fund the organizing

A local gift keeps the lights on for the chapter’s work in East Tennessee. It pays for the room we meet in, the flyers we hand out, and the mutual aid we move. Every dollar stays here, with neighbors organizing for neighbors.

Please read first

Knoxville Area DSA is a 501(c)(4) nonprofit, EIN 83-2598312. Because of that status, donations are not tax-deductible. A local gift to the chapter is also separate from national DSA membership dues: dues join you to the organization and fund the national, while a local donation funds this chapter’s day-to-day organizing right here.

Where it goes

The chapter runs lean and runs on small gifts from members and neighbors. There is no big donor and no outside money steering the work. Here is what your gift covers:

  • Room rent for our monthly General Meeting and working group spaces.
  • Printing and supplies: flyers, signs, clipboards, and the literature we hand out at actions.
  • Mutual aid: groceries, gas, and direct support for neighbors when they need it.
  • Materials for new members: welcome packets, study readings for the Little Red Schoolhouse, and orientation.
  • Keeping the chapter independent, so the work answers to members and no one else.

Pick an amount

Give once, or set it to repeat monthly. A small monthly gift is what keeps the chapter steady from one meeting to the next.

A note on dues versus a donation: paying national DSA dues makes you a member with a vote, and the chapter is funded mostly by small local gifts on top of that. The two are separate, and both help.

Membership is the bigger ask

If you only do one thing, join. A one-time gift is generous, and we are grateful for it, but a sustaining member matters more: it gives you a vote in where the chapter goes, it puts you in the room where decisions get made, and it gives the work a steady base to plan around. Money helps, but people are how we win.

Dues start where you can, and waivers are available, no questions asked. The visitor’s zip code auto-assigns to Knoxville, so joining national plugs you straight into this chapter.
