Working groups and campaigns

Find your people

The chapter does its real work in working groups. They are the on-ramp: pick the fight that pulls at you, show up once, and you are in. No experience required, and you can try more than one.

See the groups New here? Start here

How working groups work

A working group is a small standing team that owns one area of the chapter’s work. Most meet once a month, plan between meetings in a group chat, and bring proposals to the monthly General Meeting when a decision needs the whole chapter.

You do not have to be a dues-paying member to come to a working group, and you do not have to commit to anything by showing up. The fastest way in is to pick one below, fill in its interest form, and come to the next meeting. Someone will look out for you when you arrive.

What to expect at your first one

  • A quick welcome and round of introductions. You can pass.
  • A look at what the group is working on right now.
  • One concrete way to plug in before the next meeting, if you want it.

Two ways to start

Not sure which group fits? Come to a General Meeting first, or tell us what you care about and an organizer will point you to the right room.
