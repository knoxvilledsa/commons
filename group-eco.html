Working group

Eco-Socialism

Environmental health, climate, and a Green New Deal that lands in East Tennessee.

Join this group All working groups

What we do

East Tennessee lives with the consequences of decisions made for profit: coal ash ponds along the Clinch and Emory rivers, air quality that pushes asthma rates up in low-income neighborhoods, and an energy grid still shaped by a utility that answers to no voters. We organize to change that, connecting climate justice to the everyday conditions people in Knoxville are already fighting.

We are a standing team inside the chapter. We read and think together, bring issues to the public, and build the political case for a Green New Deal that actually means something here, not just a slogan from somewhere else.

What we are working on

Our focus stays rooted in what is real and local:

  • Public education on climate and environmental health in East Tennessee, including TVA’s coal ash sites and their impact on nearby communities.
  • Building local support for a Green New Deal framework: clean energy, good jobs, and environmental remediation as a package, not a trade-off.
  • Connecting environmental issues to the chapter’s broader work on housing, labor, and transit, because the people most harmed by pollution are the same people we organize with everywhere else.
  • Raising public awareness through tabling, community events, and chapter communications.

What you would actually do

You do not need a background in climate science or environmental policy. Newcomers usually start with something small and concrete:

  • Read a short piece with the group and talk through what it means for Knoxville.
  • Help staff a table at a chapter event and talk with your neighbors about local environmental issues.
  • Research a specific local issue, like air quality data or a TVA site, and share what you find.
  • Help write a flyer, draft social media copy, or take notes so the group can keep moving.
