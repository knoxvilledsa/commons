Know Your Rights: Knoxville and East Tennessee

Back to full guide
Front
  • I am exercising my right to remain silent.
  • I do not consent to a search.
  • I do not consent to you entering my home.
  • Do you have a judicial warrant signed by a judge? Slide it under the door.
  • Am I being detained, or am I free to go?
  • I want to speak with a lawyer.
  • I need an interpreter.
  • -- Do not open the door to ICE. An ICE form (I-200 / I-205) is NOT a judge's warrant.
  • -- Stay calm. Keep hands visible. Do not run. Do not lie. Do not sign ICE or legal papers without a lawyer. Signing a routine traffic or citation ticket is OK and is not an admission of guilt.
  • -- If driving: show license, registration, insurance. Passengers may stay silent.
  • -- Never claim to be a U.S. citizen if you are not, and never show false papers.
