Working group

Labor

Strike support and on-the-job organizing across East Tennessee.

Join this group All working groups

What we do

Labor is how working people change the balance of power. We organize alongside workers across East Tennessee who are fighting for better wages, safer conditions, and the right to have a say on the job. Strike support and on-the-job organizing are our two primary tools.

We are a standing team inside the chapter. We show up for workers who are in a fight, help connect isolated organizers with one another, and bring the chapter’s collective voice to labor actions when it matters.

What we are working on

This group is newer. The areas below are labeled examples of where we could focus, not current commitments.

  • Strike support: solidarity with workers who walk out, from supply runs to showing up on the picket line.
  • Service-sector organizing: connecting workers in retail, food service, and hospitality who are organizing or considering it.
  • Campus organizing at UT: supporting student workers and graduate employees in fights for recognition and fair pay.
  • Worker solidarity events: study nights, skill shares, and public forums that build the chapter’s labor base.

What you would actually do

You do not need any organizing experience, and you do not need to be in a union. Newcomers usually start with something small and concrete:

  • Show up on a picket line with the group when a strike is called nearby.
  • Help with a supply run or a solidarity table for workers in a fight.
  • Come to a meeting and say what sector you work in or know well.
  • Help write a leaflet, take photos, or keep notes so the rest of us can follow up.
