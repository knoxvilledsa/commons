Riders pack city council for better KAT service

On a rainy Tuesday night, dozens of bus riders and Knoxville Area DSA members filled the council chamber to say the same thing in a hundred ways: the buses need to come more often, run later, and reach more of the city.

June 9, 2026 / Public Transportation

This article is invented sample content, created to show how a real chapter post reads. The names and the hearing are fictional. The monthly General Meeting cadence and the Public Transportation working group are real.

By a little after six, the overflow chairs were already gone. People stood along the back wall of the City County Building, shaking the rain off their jackets, holding numbered comment cards and homemade signs that read “Frequency is freedom” and “Run the 22 on Sundays.” It was a weeknight, the kind of meeting that usually draws a handful of regulars and a reporter or two. Tuesday was different.

The reason was simple, and it is the reason public transit is the fight it is: in Knoxville, the bus is not a convenience. For a lot of our neighbors, it is the only way to get to a shift, a clinic, a grocery store, or a kid’s school. When a route runs once an hour and stops by early evening, a missed connection is not an inconvenience. It is a missed paycheck.

Knoxville Area DSA’s Public Transportation working group has spent the spring riding the system, talking with riders at stops, and mapping where the gaps fall hardest. The picture that came back was consistent. Headways on key routes stretch to an hour or more. Evening and weekend service thins out right when service workers need it most. Whole neighborhoods sit a long, unsafe walk from the nearest stop.

So members did what the chapter does best. They turned out. In the week before the hearing, volunteers signed up riders to give public comment, helped neighbors fill out comment cards, and set up a folding table outside the Knoxville Station transit center to hear directly from the people who depend on KAT every day. By the time the meeting opened, more than forty residents had signed up to speak.

“I work until ten and the last bus on my route leaves at quarter to nine,” said Renata, a rider from Lonsdale and a chapter member. “So three nights a week I am walking home in the dark, or paying for a ride I cannot really afford. That is not a choice. That is a service that quit on me.”

One after another, riders stepped to the microphone. A nursing assistant from East Knoxville described transferring twice to reach a job that is fifteen minutes away by car. A student talked about routes that do not line up with class times. The stories were specific, local, and impossible to wave off as abstractions about a budget line.

“People keep calling this a funding question, and it is,” said Marcus, a rider from Mechanicsville who came with two of his neighbors. “But it is also a dignity question. Put the money where the riders are and watch what this city can do.”

The chapter’s ask to the city was concrete. Bring the busiest routes to thirty-minute frequency. Extend evening service so the last bus does not leave before the late shifts end. Restore and expand Sunday coverage. And build the next budget with riders at the table, not as an afterthought once the numbers are already set. None of it is exotic; it is what a city that takes working people seriously would already be doing.

No vote was taken Tuesday, and no one expected one. Hearings like this are where pressure starts, not where it finishes. Council members heard, on the record and in their own chamber, that there is an organized, growing constituency for better buses in Knoxville. The next step is keeping that pressure on through the budget season, and that is work anyone reading this can join.

If transit is your fight, the Public Transportation working group meets the 2nd Monday of the month, and newcomers are always welcome. You do not need to be a member to come, and you do not need experience, just a willingness to show up. Open the Public Transportation group page to find the next meeting, or check what else is coming up on the events page.
